## üèÜ May 2025 ‚Äì Awards at the DGBP Conference  

<p style="text-align: justify;">
Several members of the <b>NeuroSTA group</b> at the Department of Psychiatry and Psychotherapy, Philipps-University Marburg, were recognized with awards at this year‚Äôs annual meeting of the German Society for Biological Psychiatry (DGBP) in Berlin, Germany.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
<b>Dr. Frederike Stein</b>, Principal Investigator of <b>NeuroSTA</b>, received the <b>DGBP Research Award for Early Career Scientists</b> for her outstanding contributions to biological psychiatry.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
Two NeuroSTA medical doctoral researchers were also honored for their poster presentations:
</p>

<ul>
  <li style="text-align: justify;">
    <b>Lea Kercher</b> received the <b>1st Poster Prize</b> for her work on the transdiagnostic analysis of delusional ideation.
  </li>
  <li style="text-align: justify;">
    <b>Lisa Koob</b> received the <b>3rd Poster Prize</b> for her research on sleep and depression.
  </li>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
The three-day conference featured a wide range of scientific talks, discussions, and networking opportunities for researchers in biological psychiatry. These awards highlight not only the quality of the presented work but also the strong commitment to supporting early-career researchers within the NeuroSTA group and at the Department of Psychiatry and Psychotherapy in Marburg.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
<b>Warm congratulations to all awardees, and sincere thanks to the DGBP for supporting early career researchers.</b>
